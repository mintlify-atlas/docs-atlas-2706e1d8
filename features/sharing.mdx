---
title: "Sharing and downloading tracks"
description: "Access and download tracks shared in your groups"
---

Every track uploaded to a group is immediately available to all group members. You can browse tracks on the group page and download any file directly to your device.

## Viewing shared tracks

Open a group from your dashboard to see its **Shared Tracks** list. Each track entry displays:

- **Track title** and **artist name**
- The email address of the member who uploaded the track (shown as *Shared by ...*)  
- The date the track was uploaded

The list is ordered by upload date, with the most recently added tracks at the top.

## Downloading a track

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the group page">
    From your dashboard, click the group that contains the track you want to download.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the track">
    Scroll through the **Shared Tracks** list to locate the track by title or artist.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Download">
    Click the **Download** button on the right side of the track row. The file is fetched from Supabase Storage and saved to your device using the filename format `{artist} - {title}.{extension}`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Recently uploaded view

Your dashboard includes a **Recently Uploaded** section that aggregates the latest tracks from all groups you belong to — not just one group at a time. Each track in this view also shows the group it came from, so you can quickly navigate to the right group for context or to find related tracks.

This view is powered by a query that joins your group memberships to the `tracks` table, orders results by `upload_date` descending, and includes the `groups.name` field alongside each track.

<Tip>
  Downloaded tracks can be added to Spotify as local files. Place the downloaded `.mp3` file in a folder that Spotify is configured to scan for local files, then enable **Local Files** in Spotify's settings. The track will appear in your local files library and can be added to any playlist — including playlists you share with friends who also have the file.
</Tip>
